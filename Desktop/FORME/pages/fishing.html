Thank you.

You remind me that there are still adventures to be had in this twisted state of the world. That mystery still exists. That questions remain unanswered, and that chance is still real. So much of life feels predetermined, like a game laid out before I even got here, and that can feel suffocating. But when I fish, I remember—the world is still unknown.

The act itself, casting into uncharted waters, searching for the beasts that lurk in the deep, reminds me that wonder isn't gone. That mystery still thrives. Traveling to new places, discovering new creatures—sometimes monsters—and witnessing both the beauty of the world and the raw reality of life and death, it all teaches me something. Even in consumption, even in the cycle of life, there is something breathtaking.

Life is not predetermined. Thank you, fishing, for showing me that I can make my own path. And who knows who I'll meet along the way?

- A Grateful Fisherman
